Kathryn Hodge

Programmer | YouTuber | Creator

Until taking an introductory course in college, I knew nothing of Computer Science, but once in the class, I was smitten. Putting little bits of code together to print out data – to make something real happen – fascinated me and I wanted to keep programming even after the labs and classes were over. In two years, I went from knowing nothing to fully immersing myself in it as a computer science teaching assistant. What if I hadn't taken that class? I never would have found out how fascinating computer science really is or discovered my true passion.

That’s why this year I created a YouTube programming tutorial series called “30 Days of Code” for people with no experience that want to learn how to code. Whether you are a 10-year-old girl at home or a grandparent wanting to know more about the inner-workings of your smart phone, this series is for anyone wanting to learn more about computer science without feeling intimidated or pressured. In teaming with a startup called HackerRank, each video tutorial is also accompanied with a HackerRank coding challenge so that viewers can put their skills to the test immediately. The series launched on December 31st, 2015 with over 20,000 signups and now has over 85,000 views on YouTube.





Today, I'm just as in love with computer science as I was two years ago. I am a junior at Vassar College majoring in Computer Science and minioring in Film and a Computer Science Teaching Assistant for Introduction to Data Structures and Algorithms. I am also the co-founder of a student-run organization called VC++, where designers, programmers, and entrepreneurs come together to create things they are passionate about, and the founder and president of VassarHackers, an on-campus group that organizes trips to hackathons throughout the Northeast.

I love problem-solving, creating tutorials, running workshops, going to hackathons and (fun fact!) tap dancing. Lack of diversity and fear of the unknown can scare away many people from engineering and computer science, but I am working to change that. I'm not sure what career I'll have in the future, but I want to be challenged everyday, never be bored, and inspire more women to try something they never thought they would be doing – computer science.
